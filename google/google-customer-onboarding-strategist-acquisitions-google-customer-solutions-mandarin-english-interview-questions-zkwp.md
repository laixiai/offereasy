*   **Points of Assessment**: This question assesses your organizational skills, time management, strategic thinking, and ability to handle multiple clients simultaneously. The interviewer wants to understand your methodology for client management and your focus on achieving measurable results.
*   **Standard Answer**: In my previous role at [Previous Company], I managed a portfolio of around 20 clients. Each week, I would dedicate specific time blocks to analyze performance data for each client, identifying key opportunities or risks. *   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving a generic answer like "I treat all clients equally." Failing to mention data-driven decision-making in prioritization. Not connecting your actions to specific, quantifiable results like growth targets.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:

    *   Tell me about a time you had to part ways with a low-potential client. How did you handle it?

### Question 2ï¼šWalk me through how you would onboard a new client who has a limited budget and is skeptical about the effectiveness of digital advertising.
*   **Points of Assessment**: This evaluates your consultative selling skills, empathy, ability to handle objections, and strategic thinking. The interviewer is looking for your ability to build trust and demonstrate value, even in challenging situations.
*   **Standard Answer**: I would start by focusing on building trust and understanding their business. To address their skepticism, I would propose a highly targeted, small-scale pilot campaign with a clear, measurable objective, like generating a specific number of leads. I would explain every step, set realistic expectations, and provide data-driven case studies from similar businesses. Throughout the pilot, I would maintain frequent communication, sharing performance data and insights. The goal is to demonstrate a tangible return on a small investment, thereby proving the value of digital advertising and building their confidence to scale up.
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Immediately jumping into a sales pitch for Google products. Dismissing the client's skepticism instead of addressing it with data and a strategic plan. Proposing a large, complex strategy that ignores their budget limitations.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What if the pilot campaign doesn't meet the initial goal? What would be your next step?
    *   How do you determine what success metrics are most important for a new client?
    *   Describe a time you successfully turned a skeptical client into a long-term partner.
