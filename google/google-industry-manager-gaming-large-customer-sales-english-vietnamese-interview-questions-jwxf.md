### Question 1ï¼šDescribe a time you developed a joint business plan with a major client. What was the process, and what was the outcome?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses strategic planning capabilities, client collaboration skills, and ability to drive long-term growth.
*   **Standard Answer**: "At my previous company, I was responsible for a key gaming account that had ambitious user acquisition goals but a scattered marketing strategy. I initiated the process for a joint business plan by first conducting a deep-dive analysis of their past performance, market position, and untapped opportunities. I then scheduled a series of workshops with their C-level stakeholders to align on a shared vision and establish clear KPIs for the next two years. The plan we co-developed focused on diversifying their ad spend across YouTube and App campaigns and leveraging Google's audience insights to target high-value players. The outcome was a 40% increase in their ROI and a 25% growth in their active user base within the first year."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving a generic answer without specifics; failing to explain how client input was incorporated; not quantifying the results of the plan.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How did you handle disagreements with the client during the planning process?
    *   What metrics did you use to define success?
    *   How did you ensure cross-functional teams on both sides were aligned with the plan?

### Question 2ï¼šTell me about a complex client problem you solved using data analysis. How did you identify the key insights?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates analytical and problem-solving skills; ability to translate data into actionable business strategy.
*   **Standard Answer**: "A major mobile game publisher was struggling with high user churn after 7 days. They believed the issue was with the game itself, but I hypothesized it was a user acquisition quality problem. I analyzed their campaign data in conjunction with their in-game analytics, segmenting users by acquisition channel. My analysis revealed that users acquired through certain Display Network campaigns had a 50% lower retention rate, despite a low CPI. The insight was that we were acquiring the wrong type of user. I presented this data to the client and recommended shifting budget to Search and YouTube campaigns targeting users with higher demonstrated intent. This led to a 30% improvement in 7-day retention and a more efficient marketing spend."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Describing the data but not the insight; failing to explain the methodology used; presenting a simple problem as complex.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What tools did you use for this analysis?
    *   How did you present this technical finding to a non-technical stakeholder?
    *   What was the most challenging part of this analysis?

### Question 3ï¼šWhat are the biggest challenges and opportunities for gaming companies in the Vietnamese market today?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Tests industry knowledge, strategic thinking, and understanding of the local market context.
*   **Standard Answer**: "The Vietnamese gaming market is incredibly dynamic. A major challenge is the rising user acquisition cost and market saturation in hyper-casual genres, forcing developers to innovate. However, this presents a huge opportunity in hybrid-casual and more complex genres with deeper monetization. Another opportunity lies in the explosive growth of esports, which opens new revenue streams through sponsorships and media rights. Furthermore, with over 70% of gamers on mobile devices, there's immense potential for companies that can master mobile-first user experiences and leverage platforms like YouTube and even TikTok for authentic community engagement. The key is to understand local player preferences and payment behaviors to truly succeed."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Giving a generic, global answer not specific to Vietnam; focusing only on challenges or only on opportunities; lacking specific data or trends to support claims.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would Google's solutions help a company navigate these challenges?
    *   Which specific game genre do you see having the most growth potential in Vietnam?
    *   How has the regulatory environment impacted the gaming industry in Vietnam?

### Question 4ï¼šHow would you position Google's advertising solutions to a gaming client looking to acquire high-value users for a new role-playing game (RPG)?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses knowledge of Google's products and the ability to apply them in a consultative sales context.
*   **Standard Answer**: "For a new RPG, acquiring high-value users who are likely to make in-app purchases is key. I would propose a multi-layered strategy. First, we'd use Google App Campaigns for broad reach, but with a specific focus on optimizing for in-app actions, not just installs. Second, I would leverage YouTube's custom affinity audiences to target channels and content related to RPGs and fantasy genres, ensuring we reach a passionate and engaged audience. Finally, we'd use Search campaigns to capture high-intent users actively looking for new RPG games. By analyzing the data from all three, we can continuously optimize the budget towards the channels that deliver the highest lifetime value users."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Listing products without explaining the strategy; not tailoring the solution to the specific game genre (RPG); forgetting to mention measurement and optimization.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you measure the success of this campaign beyond installs?
    *   What creative advice would you give the client for their YouTube ads?
    *   How would you address a client's concern about the higher CPI on Search compared to other channels?

    *   Describe a time you successfully influenced a skeptical senior leader.

### Question 7ï¼šA major gaming client wants to launch a new title but has a limited budget. Outline a creative, data-driven strategy using Google's platforms.
*   **Points of Assessment**: Probes creativity, strategic thinking, and the ability to deliver results under constraints.
*   **Standard Answer**: "With a limited budget, efficiency is paramount. I would recommend a phased approach. Phase one would be a pre-launch 'hype' campaign on YouTube, using highly engaging trailers and collaborating with a few micro-influencers in the Vietnamese gaming scene to build an initial subscriber base. We would use this to build a remarketing list. Phase two, at launch, would focus on Google App Campaigns optimized heavily for 'cost per acquisition' to ensure every dollar is spent on driving actual installs. We would use the remarketing list from phase one for targeted ads. Post-launch, I would analyze the data from the initial user cohort to identify the characteristics of the most valuable players and use those insights to refine targeting for all future campaigns, maximizing the impact of their limited budget."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Suggesting a strategy that ignores the budget constraint; focusing on only one platform; failing to incorporate a measurement and iteration loop.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How would you define the target audience for the micro-influencer campaign?
    *   How would you advise the client to allocate their budget between the pre-launch and launch phases?
    *   What free Google tools could the client use to supplement this strategy?

### Question 8ï¼šA client is seeing better ROI from a competitor's platform and is considering shifting their budget. How would you handle this objection?
*   **Points of Assessment**: Evaluates objection handling, competitive awareness, and customer retention skills.
*   **Standard Answer**: "My first step would be to listen and validate their concern, not to get defensive. I would ask them to walk me through their data and methodology for calculating ROI to ensure we're making an apples-to-apples comparison. It's possible they are measuring success differently on each platform. Next, I would conduct a comprehensive audit of their Google Ads account to identify areas for optimization that we may have missed. I would also highlight the unique value Google offers beyond direct ROI, such as deeper audience insights and brand-building opportunities on YouTube. My goal wouldn't be to discredit the competitor, but to reframe the value proposition of Google and build a data-backed plan to improve performance and win back their confidence."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Immediately criticizing the competitor; making promises that can't be kept; failing to ask clarifying questions about the data.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   What specific value propositions of Google would you emphasize?
    *   What if the competitor's data is correct and they are outperforming Google?
    *   Describe a time you successfully retained a client who was about to leave.

### Question 9ï¼šDescribe your experience working cross-functionally with internal teams like product specialists or analysts to deliver a client solution.
*   **Points of Assessment**: Assesses teamwork, collaboration, and the ability to leverage internal resources effectively.
*   **Standard Answer**: "In my last role, I had a client facing a highly technical challenge with ad tracking and attribution. I am not a technical expert in that specific area, so I acted as the project lead. I brought in our technical product specialist and a data analyst to form a small task force. My role was to translate the client's business needs into technical requirements for the team and to manage the communication back to the client in a clear, non-technical way. By facilitating collaboration and ensuring everyone was aligned on the end goal, the team was able to design and implement a custom solution in three weeks. This not only solved the client's problem but also strengthened their trust in our company's ability to support them."
*   **Common Pitfalls**: Describing a situation where you worked alone; focusing only on your own contribution; not explaining the roles of other team members.
*   **Potential Follow-up Questions**:
    *   How do you handle conflicts or disagreements within a cross-functional team?
    *   How do you ensure that the solution developed by a technical team meets the client's strategic needs?
    *   What is your preferred style of communication when working with internal teams?
